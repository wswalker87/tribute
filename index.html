George W. Bush

The Man Who took America through 9/11
President of the US, George W. Bush.
1946
Born in New Haven Connecticut.
1948
George H.W. Bush moved the family to Midland, Texas where they made their fortune.
1961
Moved to Houston. George left Houston and went to Philips Academy in Andover, Massachusetts.
1964
Despite only being a fair student with a reputation of a troublemaker, George was accepted into Yale.
1968
Two weeks before his draft deferment, George enlisted in the Texas Air National Guard. This was the height of the Vietnam War. He was commissioned as a 2nd LT.
1970
Earned his fighter pilot certification.
1974
Despite irregular attendance, George was honorably discharged on Nov. 21st.
1975
Earned his MBA from Harvard. He then returned to Midland to enter the oil business, working for a friend. He then started his own company.
1977
At a backyard BBQ, George met Laura Welch. After a quick three month courtship they married on Novemeber 5th.
1981
Twin daughters, Barbara and Jenna, born in Midland, Texas.
1986
Sold his struggling business to Harken Energy Corporation in exchange for stock and a seat on the board.
1988
Moved to Washington, DC to help with his father's Presidental election. After his father's victory he moved back to Texas
1989
Invested with a group into the Texas Rangers team. Became the de facto leader of the group and earned a reputation as a savvy businessman. In 1998, when he sold of his part of the team, he received 17 times his initiat investment.
1994
After his father's 1992 loss to Bill Clinton, George decided to run for governor of Texas. He won out over Democratic incumbant Ann Richards.
1998
Became the first person to serve as Texas Governor for two consecutive four year terms.
1999
Began his chase for the Presidency. He got the Republican nomination in 2000, up against Democratic candidate, Al Gore.
2001
On September 11th, after the terrorist attacks on the Twin Towers, Bush jumped into action.
2004
Despite the war not going well and his social security plans not going well, Bush won his re-election.
2009
Bush left office with extremely low approval ratings and a large amount of unfinished business. He and Laura relocated to Dallas, Texas.
2010
Bush joined former president Clinton and President Obama in a fundraiser for Haiti.
2013
Bush opened his memorial library. In July of that year, he made history by meeting President Obama in Africa to commemorate the 15th anniversary of Bin Laden's first attack on the US. Bush also began to undergo some heart issues, resulting in a stent and multiple hospital visits.
